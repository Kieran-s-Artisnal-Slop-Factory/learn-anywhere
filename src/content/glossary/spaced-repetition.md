---
term: Spaced repetition
short: Reviewing material at increasing intervals — just before you would forget it — instead of cramming it all at once.
---

Spaced repetition schedules reviews at growing intervals: a day after
learning, then three days, then a week, then a month. Each successful review
happens right when the memory is starting to fade, which is exactly when a
review does the most good.

## Why it works

Forgetting isn't a failure — it's the trigger that makes re-learning stick.
The harder (but still successful) a retrieval is, the more it strengthens the
memory. Cramming feels productive because everything is fresh, but the
retention curve collapses within days.

## In practice

- Flashcard apps automate the scheduling for you.
- Without an app: review new material after 1 day, 3 days, 1 week, 2 weeks.
- Combine with [[active-recall|active recall]] — spaced *re-reading* is far weaker than
  spaced *retrieval*.
