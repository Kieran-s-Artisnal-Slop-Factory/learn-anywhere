---
term: Active recall
short: Deliberately retrieving information from memory (instead of re-reading it), which strengthens the memory far more than passive review.
---

Active recall is the practice of testing yourself on material instead of
re-reading or re-watching it. Every time you force your brain to retrieve a
fact, the retrieval path gets stronger — an effect that decades of research
consistently finds to be one of the most powerful study techniques available.

## How to use it

- Close the book and write down everything you remember.
- Turn headings into questions and answer them before reading the section.
- Use flashcards — but only if you genuinely try to answer before flipping.

Active recall pairs naturally with [[spaced-repetition|spaced repetition]]: recall strengthens
the memory, spacing schedules the next recall for the moment it's about to
fade.
