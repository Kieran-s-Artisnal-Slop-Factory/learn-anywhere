---
term: Interleaving
short: Mixing different topics or problem types within one study session, rather than practicing one kind of problem in a long block.
---

Interleaving means shuffling topics during practice: instead of doing twenty
problems of type A, then twenty of type B (blocked practice), you mix
A-B-C problems throughout the session.

## Why it works

Real tests — and real life — don't tell you which technique a problem needs.
Interleaved practice forces you to *choose* the right approach, not just
execute it, which builds the discrimination skill blocked practice never
touches. It feels harder and slower while studying, and that difficulty is
precisely what makes it stick.

## In practice

- Mix problem types within a homework session.
- Rotate subjects across the week in shorter sessions, spaced apart like
  [[spaced-repetition|spaced repetition]] recommends.
