---
term: Mnemonic
short: A memory device — an acronym, rhyme, image, or story — that packages hard-to-remember information into an easy-to-remember form.
---

A mnemonic is any device that converts arbitrary information into something
memorable: "Every Good Boy Deserves Fudge" for the lines of the treble clef,
a vivid mental image linking a name to a face, or a story that strings a
shopping list together.

## Why they work

Human memory is excellent at images, places, and stories, and poor at
abstract lists. A mnemonic borrows the strong systems to carry the weak
material — and by packing several items into one chunk, it also eases the
load on [[working-memory|working memory]].

## Limits

Mnemonics are for *retention*, not *understanding*. They shine for ordered
lists, vocabularies, and formulas; they can't replace actually grasping how
ideas connect.
