---
term: Working memory
short: The small mental workspace that holds what you're thinking about right now — typically only about four chunks at a time.
---

Working memory is the mental scratchpad where conscious thinking happens.
It's severely limited: most people can hold roughly four chunks of new
information at once. When a lesson feels overwhelming, that's usually working
memory overflowing — not a lack of ability.

## Working with the limit

- Break material into small pieces and master them one at a time.
- Chunk related facts into a single meaningful unit (a [[mnemonic|mnemonic]] does this).
- Offload aggressively: write things down instead of juggling them mentally.

Long-term memory has no practical limit — the goal of studying is to move
knowledge out of the crowded workspace and into permanent storage, where it
can be pulled back with [[active-recall|active recall]].
