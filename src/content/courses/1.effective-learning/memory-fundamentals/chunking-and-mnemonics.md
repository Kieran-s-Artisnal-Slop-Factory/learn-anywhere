If [[working-memory|working memory]] can only hold four things, how does anyone think about
anything complicated? The answer is **chunking**: what counts as "one thing"
is up to you.

## Chunking

A phone number is ten digits — or three chunks. A chess master doesn't see
six pieces; they see "a castled king". Expertise largely *is* chunking:
compressing many details into single meaningful units so the workspace can
hold more.

## Mnemonics

A [[mnemonic|mnemonic]] is deliberate chunking plus memorable packaging: an acronym,
a rhyme, a vivid image, a story. They work because human memory is superb at
images, places, and narratives and terrible at arbitrary lists — so you let
the strong system carry the weak material.

Use them for ordered lists, vocabulary, and formulas. Don't expect them to
substitute for understanding — that's what the rest of this course is for.
