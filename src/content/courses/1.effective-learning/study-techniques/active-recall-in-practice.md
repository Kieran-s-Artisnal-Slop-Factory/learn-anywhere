[[active-recall|Active recall]] is the single highest-leverage change most people can make
to how they study. The principle is one sentence: **retrieve, don't review**.

## Making review active

- **Brain dump.** Close the book, write everything you remember, then check
  what you missed. The misses are your study list.
- **Questions from headings.** Turn every heading into a question before
  reading the section, then answer it after — and again the next day.
- **Honest flashcards.** A flashcard only works if you commit to an answer
  before flipping it. The flip-fast shuffle is just re-reading in disguise.

## Why it feels wrong

Retrieval is effortful and full of visible failures, while re-reading is
smooth and comfortable. Your sense of fluency is measuring the comfort, not
the learning. Trust the quiz results over the feeling — which is exactly why
lessons here end with one.
