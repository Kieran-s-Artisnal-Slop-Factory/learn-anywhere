Time to turn technique into routine. A good plan is boring on purpose — small
sessions, fixed slots, and self-testing built in.

## The template

1. **List your subjects** and rank them by how soon and how hard they'll be
   tested.
2. **Slot short sessions** (25–50 minutes) across the week. Two spaced
   sessions beat one double-length session — that's [[spaced-repetition|spaced repetition]]
   working for free.
3. **Mix within sessions.** Rotate two or three topics per sitting
   ([[interleaving|interleaving]]), especially for problem-heavy subjects.
4. **End every session with retrieval.** Five minutes of brain dump or
   self-quizzing ([[active-recall|active recall]]) is worth more than fifteen minutes of
   extra reading.
5. **Schedule reviews, not just first passes.** New material gets a review
   slot at ~1 day, ~3 days, and ~1 week.
