---
title: Study Planning Facts
cards:
  - front: How long should a focused study session be?
    back: 25–50 minutes — long enough for depth, short enough to stay focused.
  - front: When should new material get its first review?
    back: About one day after first learning it, then ~3 days, then ~1 week.
  - front: What should the last five minutes of every study session be?
    back: Retrieval — a brain dump or self-quiz, not more reading.
  - front: Two spaced one-hour sessions vs. one two-hour session — which wins?
    back: The two spaced sessions, by a wide margin, for long-term retention.
  - front: Why does interleaved practice feel worse while you do it?
    back: Because choosing the right approach for each problem is harder than repeating one approach — and that added difficulty is what makes it stick.
---

Quick-fire facts for building and sticking to a study plan, drawn from the
Study Techniques chapter.
